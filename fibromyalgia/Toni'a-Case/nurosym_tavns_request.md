# Patient Request: Trial of Non-Invasive Vagus Nerve Stimulation (taVNS)

**Adding a non-invasive vagus nerve stimulation device to my fibromyalgia management. Here's what it is, why I think it fits me, and what I'm asking you to do.**

## WHAT IS IT?

*   **Device:** Nurosym™ — a small ear clip worn on the left ear
*   **What it does:** Sends gentle electrical pulses to the vagus nerve through the skin (called "taVNS" — transcutaneous auricular vagus nerve stimulation)
*   **Regulatory:** CE-Marked medical device (EU) + FDA Non-Significant Risk (NSR)
*   **Availability:** Over-the-counter. No prescription needed. I can buy it myself.
*   **Protocol:** 30 minutes, twice a day, for 28 days

## WHY I WANT TO TRY IT

*   I have documented paradoxical/excitatory reactions to multiple medications. I need something that isn't a drug.
*   My main symptom is the "internal earthquake" — constant internal tremor, buzzing, sensory static. This is autonomic dysregulation + central sensitization, not just "pain."
*   Vagus nerve stimulation calms the autonomic nervous system and reduces inflammatory cytokines (TNF-α, IL-6, IL-8) — the same ones now linked to fibromyalgia in 2025-2026 research.
*   A clinical trial was done on women MY AGE with MY DIAGNOSIS using THIS EXACT DEVICE. It worked. (See papers below.)

*"Non-invasive auricular vagus nerve stimulation in fibromyalgia."*

### PAPER 2 — The Systematic Review (December 2025)

Perin, Pastora-Sesín, et al.
*"Potential of Vagus Nerve Stimulation to Modulate Fibromyalgia's Network Physiology: A Systematic Review."*
J Funct Morphol Kinesiol. Dec 29, 2025; 11(1):15.
Harvard Medical School / Spaulding Rehabilitation Hospital

*   Reviewed 304 records, included 6 studies, 208 total patients
*   **Conclusion:** taVNS is "a promising tool for mechanism-based therapeutics in central sensitization syndromes"

*   **PMC:** https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12821727/
